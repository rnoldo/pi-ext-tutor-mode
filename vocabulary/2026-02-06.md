# Vocabulary - 2026-02-06

## walk someone through
- **User's version:** "What did you do" / "tell me"
- **Meaning:** To explain a process or set of steps in detail to someone
- **Example:** "Can you walk me through the logic in this function?"

## best practices for working with
- **User's version:** "what's the best practice to work with you"
- **Meaning:** The most effective or efficient way to collaborate
- **Example:** "What are the best practices for working with legacy code?"

## give a brief intro
- **User's version:** "introduce yourself to me a little bit"
- **Meaning:** A common, natural way to ask for a short introduction
- **Example:** "Could you give us a brief intro before we start?"

## switch between modes
- **User's version:** "swith between learn english and work fast"
- **Meaning:** To change from one state or method of operation to another
- **Example:** "I need a way to easily switch between development and production modes."

## seamlessly
- **User's version:** "convinently"
- **Meaning:** Smoothly and continuously, without any awkward transition or interruption

## natural language
- **User's version:** "nature language"
- **Meaning:** Ordinary language that humans use to communicate (as opposed to computer code)
- **Example:** "The AI can process complex queries in natural language."

## persist
- **User's version:** "does this still work" (in context of future/restart)
- **Meaning:** To continue to exist or apply, especially across different sessions or restarts
- **Example:** "Will my configuration persist after I reboot the server?"
